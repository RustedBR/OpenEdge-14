# Species & Races Guide

Choose your race carefully! Each species in OpenEdge 14 has unique advantages and roleplay opportunities.

## Available Species

### 1. Human

**The Balanced Choice**

The most versatile race with no penalties and modest bonuses. Humans excel at any profession.

**Stats Modifiers**
- Health: +5%
- Mana: No change
- Strength: +0
- Dexterity: +0
- Intelligence: +0
- Wisdom: +0

**Special Traits**
- Can learn any craft equally well
- Natural leader (+10% leadership skill)
- Bonus job experience (+5%)

**Roleplay Notes**
- From all walks of life
- Adaptable and diverse
- The "default" choice - good for new players

**Good Jobs**
- Guard (natural defenders)
- Merchant (traders)
- Adventurer (all-rounders)

---

### 2. Elf

**The Graceful Warriors**

Tall, elegant beings with affinity for magic and precision combat.

**Stats Modifiers**
- Health: -10
- Mana: +20
- Strength: -1
- Dexterity: +3
- Intelligence: +2
- Wisdom: +1

**Special Traits**
- Magic Affinity: +20% spell damage
- Precision Strikes: +15% accuracy with bows
- Night Vision: Can see in dark conditions
- Lower food consumption

**Weaknesses**
- Less health than humans
- Susceptible to cold (take extra cold damage)

**Roleplay Notes**
- Proud and magical
- Long-lived (centuries)
- Often connected to nature
- Formal speech patterns

**Good Jobs**
- Alchemist (magical affinity)
- Adventurer (archery skills)
- Guard (precision fighting)

---

### 3. Dwarf

**The Sturdy Crafters**

Short, stocky beings with incredible durability and crafting knowledge.

**Stats Modifiers**
- Health: +30
- Mana: -10
- Strength: +2
- Dexterity: -2
- Intelligence: +1
- Wisdom: +2

**Special Traits**
- Stone Mastery: +30% mining speed
- Crafting Expertise: -20% crafting time
- Alcohol Tolerance: 50% resistance to intoxication
- Natural Armor: +15% damage reduction

**Weaknesses**
- Slower movement (90% speed)
- Less mana (fewer spells)

**Roleplay Notes**
- Hardworking and pragmatic
- Love gold and crafted items
- Deep voices and gruff manners
- Excellent memory for distances and maps

**Good Jobs**
- Smithy (crafting excellence)
- Guard (high durability)
- Merchant (wealth-focused)

---

### 4. Tiefling

**The Infernal Mystics**

Descendants of demonic heritage with natural magical talent and mysterious power.

**Stats Modifiers**
- Health: -5
- Mana: +30
- Strength: +1
- Dexterity: +1
- Intelligence: +3
- Wisdom: -1

**Special Traits**
- Infernal Magic: +25% spell power, especially fire/shadow spells
- Dark Pact: Can cast spells at reduced mana cost (-10%)
- Hellfire Resistance: 30% fire damage reduction
- Summon Familiars: Can summon magical creatures (level dependent)

**Weaknesses**
- Less health
- Lower wisdom (easier to fool)
- Holy water burns them (increased damage)

**Roleplay Notes**
- Often misunderstood or feared
- Philosophical about their nature
- Dramatic and intense
- Interested in forbidden knowledge

**Good Jobs**
- Alchemist (spell-focused)
- Adventurer (magical combat)
- Scholar (knowledge-seeking)

---

### 5. Silva

**The Forest Guardians**

Plant-like humanoids with natural healing and environmental control.

**Stats Modifiers**
- Health: +10
- Mana: +15
- Strength: +1
- Dexterity: +1
- Intelligence: -1
- Wisdom: +2

**Special Traits**
- Natural Healing: Regenerate 1 HP per 5 seconds
- Green Thumb: Crops grow 50% faster
- Environmental Control: Can manipulate plants (vines, roots)
- Photosynthesis: Restore mana in sunlight (+2 mana/second)

**Weaknesses**
- Vulnerable to fire (doubled damage)
- Need sunlight to restore mana
- Slower in urban environments

**Roleplay Notes**
- Nature-focused and philosophical
- Slow to anger but fierce in defense
- Speak in measured, calm tones
- Connected to the land

**Good Jobs**
- Farmer (crop affinity)
- Alchemist (herbalism)
- Adventurer (explorer)

---

### 6. Carcat

**The Nimble Tricksters**

Feline humanoids with agility, stealth, and natural predatory instincts.

**Stats Modifiers**
- Health: -5
- Mana: No change
- Strength: -1
- Dexterity: +4
- Intelligence: +1
- Wisdom: No change

**Special Traits**
- Feline Agility: +40% movement speed, +20% jump height
- Night Hunter: +30% accuracy in darkness
- Stealth Mastery: +25% stealth ability
- Pounce Attack: Can leap into combat attacks
- Keen Senses: Better detection of hidden things

**Weaknesses**
- Lower health
- Weakness to water (move slower when wet)
- Distracted by shiny objects (roleplay mechanic)

**Roleplay Notes**
- Curious and playful
- Honor-bound to contracts
- Quick reflexes and sharp wit
- Appreciate fine foods and comfort

**Good Jobs**
- Adventurer (mobility)
- Thief/Rogue (stealth)
- Guard (quick reactions)

---

### 7. Skeleton

**The Undead Warriors**

Animated bones held together by magic, with immunity to pain and unique abilities.

**Stats Modifiers**
- Health: +20
- Mana: +10
- Strength: +2
- Dexterity: No change
- Intelligence: No change
- Wisdom: -2

**Special Traits**
- Undead: Immune to poison, disease, and bleeding
- No Pain: Cannot be slowed by pain effects
- Bone Strength: +15% weapon damage
- Restore Bones: Can repair bones (self-healing)
- Terrifying Presence: Weak enemies flee (-15% enemy morale)

**Weaknesses**
- Reviled by religious communities
- Fragile to holy damage (+50% damage taken)
- Cannot swim (sink like stone)
- Slower intelligence (reasoning penalty)

**Roleplay Notes**
- Mysterious origins
- Often solitary or tragic
- Speak in echoing, hollow tones
- Focused on their purpose

**Good Jobs**
- Guard (high durability)
- Adventurer (undead nature)
- Warrior (strength)

---

### 8. Zombie

**The Resilient Undead**

Shambling corpses infused with life essence, slow but incredibly tough.

**Stats Modifiers**
- Health: +50 (highest!)
- Mana: No change
- Strength: +3
- Dexterity: -2
- Intelligence: -2
- Wisdom: -2

**Special Traits**
- Regeneration: Heal 0.5 HP per second
- Undead Resilience: +30% damage reduction
- Disease Immunity: Cannot be poisoned or diseased
- Decay Aura: Nearby enemies take 1 damage/second
- Infection: Melee attacks can infect enemies with plague

**Weaknesses**
- Very slow movement (70% speed)
- Lower intelligence and wisdom
- Repulsive to living creatures (NPCs avoid you)
- Fire deals extra damage (+50%)

**Roleplay Notes**
- Ponderous and deliberate
- Single-minded in purpose
- Speak rarely and slowly
- Mysterious motivations

**Good Jobs**
- Guard (unkillable tank)
- Warrior (raw damage)
- Laborer (heavy work)

## Stat Explained

- **Health**: Hit points - how much damage you can take
- **Mana**: Magic pool - needed to cast spells
- **Strength**: Physical power - melee damage, carrying capacity
- **Dexterity**: Agility - accuracy, speed, stealth
- **Intelligence**: Reasoning - spell power, crafting efficiency
- **Wisdom**: Awareness - detect traps, resist mind control

## Choosing Your Species

**Tips for New Players**
1. Choose Human first - learn the game mechanics
2. Pick based on desired job - each race suits different roles
3. Consider roleplay - what personality appeals to you?
4. Test multiple species - they play very differently

**Tips for Experienced Players**
1. Use weaknesses as roleplay opportunities
2. Optimize race-job combinations for maximum efficiency
3. Create diverse characters to experience all playstyles
4. Remember: roleplay matters as much as stats!

## Interspecies Relations

### Tensions
- **Elves vs Humans**: Humans consider elves aloof; elves see humans as short-lived
- **Dwarves vs Elves**: Ancient rivalry over mountain vs forest lands
- **Living vs Undead**: Religious prejudice against skeletons and zombies
- **Tieflings vs Holy Orders**: Suspicion of infernal nature

### Alliances
- **Silva & Dwarves**: Both respect craftsmanship
- **Carcat & Humans**: Mutual respect and trade
- **Tieflings & Elves**: Both appreciate magic
- **Skeletons & Zombies**: Undead solidarity

## Roleplaying Tips

### Elf
*"We have walked these lands for centuries. Your brief lives amuse us."*
- Speak formally and with pride
- Reference history and long-term goals
- Maintain dignity in all situations

### Dwarf
*"By the Forge! That's a fine beard you're sportin'!"*
- Use hearty, gruff speech
- Value work and craftsmanship
- Enjoy ale and good company

### Tiefling
*"They fear what they don't understand. I've made peace with that."*
- Be philosophical about prejudice
- Show hidden depths and complexity
- Speak with intensity

### Carcat
*"Shiny! What's that? Can I have it?"*
- Be playful and curious
- Show cat-like behaviors (stretching, grooming)
- Respect contracts and agreements

### Silva
*"The forest speaks to those who listen."*
- Use nature metaphors
- Speak slowly and thoughtfully
- Show concern for plants and environment

### Skeleton/Zombie
*"I... remember... life..."*
- Speak rarely, in short sentences
- Show tragic nature
- Focus on singular purpose

**Remember**: Species affects gameplay, but roleplay is what makes the game fun! Embrace your character's unique perspective.
